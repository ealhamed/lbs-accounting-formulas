Cash Collected from Customers

The exam favourite from Module 4 — how to find actual cash when you only have accrual numbers

The problem: The Income Statement reports Revenue — but revenue isn't the same as cash. Some customers pay later (receivables go up), some pay in advance (deferred revenue goes up). This formula bridges the gap.
The Formula
Cash Collected = Revenue + ΔDeferred Revenue − ΔReceivables
Recognised on the Income Statement
+ means customers owe you more
+ means you collected cash upfront

Income Statement (extract)

Revenue (accrual basis) $500
Recognised when earned, not when cash received
Expenses -$300
Net Income$200

Balance Sheet (changes)

Assets

Cash+$450
Receivables (IOUs) +$80
Customers owe you $80 more than before

Liabilities

Deferred Rev (prepaid) +$30
Collected $30 for services not yet delivered
⬇ Revenue was $500 — but how much cash did customers actually hand you?

Cash Collected from Customers

Revenue (from Income Statement) $500
+ Δ Deferred Revenue (cash collected upfront) +$30
Customers prepaid — you got cash but haven't earned it yet
− Δ Receivables (earned but not collected) -$80
You earned it on paper, but the cash hasn't arrived
Cash Collected $450
Cash Collected = $450
$500 + $30 − $80 = $450
Why does this work?
Revenue tells you what was earned. But two things create a gap between earned and collected:
Δ Receivables up = you earned it but customers haven't paid yet → subtract
Δ Deferred Revenue up = customers paid you for stuff you haven't earned yet → add
The formula reverses both to get back to actual cash.

Try this

Set both deltas to 0
Cash collected = Revenue exactly. No timing differences — everyone paid on time, no prepayments. This is the simplest case.
Set ΔReceivables to +$200
Cash collected drops to $300 despite $500 revenue. $200 is stuck in IOUs — you earned it but customers haven't paid. This is why profitable companies can run out of cash.
Set ΔDeferred Revenue to +$200
Cash collected jumps to $700 — more than revenue! Customers prepaid $200 for future services. Cash is great but you still owe them the work. Think subscriptions.
Set ΔReceivables to -$50
Cash collected = $550, above revenue. You collected old debts — customers paid what they owed from last period. Receivables going down is good for cash.
Reset to defaults
Back to the starting numbers.
